Maybe one is assuming that the function is increasing and the other decreasing? Or something to do with numerical stability? Anyways, let's see what happens when h goes to 0. The limit when h goes to zero of the two expressions are as follows: $$ \lim h \rightarrow 0, x_{k + 1} = (-2 \sqrt{-h^2-2 h x_k +1}-h x_k +2)/h = \lim h \rightarrow 0, {{\delta \over \delta h}(-2 \sqrt{-h^2-2 h x_k +1}-h x_k +2) \over 1} = \lim h \rightarrow 0, {2(h+x_k ) \over \sqrt{-h^2-2 h x_k +1}}-x_k = 2x_k -x_k = x_k $$ and $$ \lim h \rightarrow 0, x_{k + 1} = (-2 \sqrt{-h^2-2 h x_k +1}-h x_k +2)/h = \lim h \rightarrow 0, {{\delta \over \delta h}(2 \sqrt{-h^2-2 h x_k +1}-h x_k +2) \over 1} = \lim h \rightarrow 0, {-2(h+x_k ) \over \sqrt{-h^2-2 h x_k +1}}-x_k = -2x_k -x_k = -3x_k $$. Why should the limit be $$x_k$$? Because it is the previous value, so when the delta is zero, we keep the previous value.
